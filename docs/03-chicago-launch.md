# Muzgram — Chicago Metro Launch Strategy

> Last updated: 2026-04-21 (expanded from Devon Ave–only to full Chicago metro)
> Market: Chicago Metropolitan Area — all 400,000+ Muslims across city + suburbs
> Philosophy: **The app finds the user. The user doesn't have to go to Devon Ave.**

---

## The Correction

The original plan anchored everything to Devon Ave. That was wrong.

Devon Ave is a *destination* — Muslims from Bridgeview drive 30 minutes there for dinner. Muslims in Schaumburg drive 40 minutes there for Eid shopping. But those same people also want halal food near their home. They want to find the Friday prayer closest to their office in Naperville. They want to see what community events are happening in their own suburb this weekend.

Muzgram's feed is GPS-driven. When a user in Bridgeview opens the app, they should see Bridgeview restaurants, Mosque Foundation events, and Arab-owned businesses in their neighborhood. They should not have to scroll past Devon Ave content to find it.

**Devon Ave is the seed cluster — not the product boundary.**

## The Revised Launch Plan — All Clusters, Day 1

The new plan is NOT sequential. Seed all clusters before launch. The app is GPS-driven. A user in Bridgeview should open Muzgram on launch day and see Bridgeview content, not Devon Ave content.

### Phase 0 — Pre-Launch Seeding (Weeks -8 to -1)
**Goal: Every major cluster has minimum viable content before the first user downloads.**

**Minimum viable content per cluster:**

| Cluster | Min Businesses | Min Events | Min Posts |
|---|---|---|---|
| Devon Ave / North Side | 40+ | 5+ | 10+ |
| Bridgeview / SW Suburbs | 20+ | 5+ | 5+ |
| Skokie / North Suburbs | 15+ | 3+ | 3+ |
| Schaumburg / NW Suburbs | 20+ | 3+ | 3+ |
| Bolingbrook / Aurora / Naperville | 15+ | 3+ | 3+ |
| South Side | 10+ | 2+ | 2+ |
| **Total at launch** | **120+** | **21+** | **26+** |

**How to seed without staff:**
The admin Data Import Manager (`docs/12-admin-dashboard.md`) handles bulk import via spreadsheet. Devon Ave is seeded by founder in-person. All other clusters are seeded via:
1. **Google Maps scraping → Import sheet** — search "halal restaurant [neighborhood]", collect addresses, import via admin spreadsheet importer, geocode all at once, set status = active. 2–3 hours per cluster.
2. **Community partner pre-seeding** — Mosque Foundation submits their own events before launch. IMAN submits their events. These are power users who self-populate.
3. **Data Import grid** — founder or volunteer fills in each cluster's businesses in the spreadsheet view, geocodes batch, imports directly to DB in one shot.

**Important:** Businesses seeded this way are set to `is_claimed = false`. The "Claim this business" button appears on every unclaimed listing — this is how organic supply-side growth starts. Businesses find their listing, claim it, and start maintaining it.

## The Key Realization About Content vs Location

Devon Ave works for Muzgram because it is:
1. A destination (people from all over Chicago go there)
2. A neighborhood (people who live there want local content)

Most of Chicago metro's Muslim communities are #2 only — neighborhoods, not destinations. A Muslim in Bolingbrook doesn't need to see Devon Ave content. They need to see:
- The halal restaurant on Ogden Ave that's open tonight
- The community iftar at their local mosque next Friday
- The Pakistani accountant three blocks from their house

The feed algorithm already handles this — it's proximity-sorted. The only missing piece was seeding content in all neighborhoods before launch, not just Devon Ave.

**The risk of Devon Ave–only seeding:**
A user in Bridgeview opens the app, sees no nearby content (or Devon Ave content 20 miles away), and deletes it immediately. They never come back. Their neighbors hear "don't bother, nothing near us." The Bridgeview market is poisoned before it starts.

**The reward of metro-wide seeding:**
A user in Bridgeview opens the app, sees 15 local businesses and 3 upcoming mosque events within 2 miles. They share it to their WhatsApp group. By end of day, 50 Bridgeview Muslims have downloaded it. Mosque Foundation posts their events. 10 Bridgeview businesses claim their listing.
