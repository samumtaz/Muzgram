# Muzgram — Brand Identity & Product Positioning

> Last updated: 2026-04-21
> This document governs every word, feature name, notification copy, onboarding screen, and product decision.
> When in doubt about tone, come back here first.

---

## The One-Sentence Identity

**Muzgram is where young Muslims find their scene — the best spots, the right events, and their people — without explaining themselves to an app that wasn't built for them.**

Not: "A halal restaurant discovery app."
Not: "An Islamic community platform."
Not: "A Muslim lifestyle app."

Those descriptions are technically accurate and completely wrong for the brand. They describe the infrastructure, not the experience. Muzgram is not a Muslim version of something else. It is the first thing that was built specifically for this generation.

---

## Who Muzgram Is For

The user is **25 years old. Born here. Culturally Muslim. Socially active.**

They eat halal not because an app told them to — they just do, it's who they are. They don't need a lecture about halal certification tiers. They need the app to quietly know that the restaurant they're considering is the right kind of place, and surface it without making a big deal about it.

They go to events. Not always mosque events — sometimes yes, mostly cultural nights, Eid parties, Muslim professional mixers, food festivals, community fundraisers, sports leagues, and late-night dessert runs. They want to know what's happening near them that their crew would actually go to.

They moved to a new city or a new neighborhood and they're trying to rebuild their social circle. They grew up in a tight Muslim community and they miss having that automatically. Muzgram is how they find it in a new place, faster.

They are not the person who needs help finding a mosque. They know where the mosque is. They need help finding where to eat after Jummah, what's happening Saturday night that isn't a wedding, and which coffee shop near their office is actually good.

**They are not a religious demographic. They are a cultural one.**

## Brand Voice

Muzgram speaks like the most connected person in the Muslim community in your city. They know every spot, every event, and everyone who matters — and they're genuinely excited to tell you about it. Not in a formal way. Not in a religious-lecture way. In the way your best-connected friend texts you.

## Content Taxonomy (Reframed)

Previous docs categorized content as "Food / Events / Services / Community." This taxonomy works technically but reads sterile. The real experience taxonomy is:

### Eat
Restaurants, cafés, bakeries, dessert spots, food trucks, grocery stores, catering. The halal filter is always applied — it's infrastructure, not the headline. Users don't think "I want to find a halal restaurant." They think "I want nihari." The app quietly ensures everything shown is the right kind of place.

Sub-vibes within Eat:
- **Late night** — open after 11pm (hookah lounges, 24-hour dives, Ramadan spots)
- **Coffee & study** — Muslim-friendly work-from-café spots
- **Family sit-down** — table service, good for bringing parents
- **Quick & close** — fast, nearby, open now
- **Treat yourself** — desserts, specialty, splurge

### Go Out
Events, parties, cultural nights, DJ sets, concerts, mixers, comedy shows, sports, markets, pop-ups — the full social calendar of a generation that is Muslim by identity and everything else by taste.

**The scope is deliberately wide.** The "Go Out" category is not limited to religious or community events. A desi party in Bridgeview is on Muzgram. A Bollywood DJ night in Schaumburg is on Muzgram. An Arab dabke cultural night in Lombard is on Muzgram. A Pakistani stand-up comedy show is on Muzgram. A halal-friendly concert venue is on Muzgram. The unifying thread is not religion — it's that people from this cultural background will go, and they want to know about it.

**Why this matters for the product:** The user who is culturally Muslim also wants to know about the desi party this Friday, the Bollywood night at that venue in the suburbs, the Arab cultural festival, and the comedy show headlined by a desi comedian. If Muzgram doesn't show them that, Instagram fills the gap. We can't let that happen.

**The filter:** Events on Muzgram are culturally relevant, not religiously filtered. A concert at a fully licensed bar does not get listed — not because of an alcohol policy, but because it's not where this crowd goes. A halal-friendly venue with a DJ, zero alcohol, and a desi crowd is exactly what gets listed.

**Sub-vibes within Go Out:**

- **Parties & Nightlife** — desi parties, Arab nights, cultural club nights, halal-friendly DJ events, late-night mixers. The weekend move.
- **Concerts & Live Music** — desi artists, Arab pop, Pakistani/Indian concert tours, Bollywood live shows, Muslim artists in general.
- **Bollywood & Desi Nights** — dedicated Bollywood DJ nights, Bhangra events, desi pop culture nights, retro Bollywood evenings.
- **Comedy & Entertainment** — desi stand-up comedy, Arab comedy tours, open mic nights, cultural entertainment shows.
- **Cultural Festivals** — Eid celebrations, South Asian cultural festivals, Arab cultural events, dabke nights, Mela events, Diwali-adjacent events the community attends.
- **Professional & Networking** — career mixers, young professional events, Muslim business conferences, CIOGC, alumni networks.
- **Sports & Active** — cricket leagues, soccer tournaments, basketball, fitness classes, outdoors events.
- **Arts & Culture** — gallery openings, poetry nights, spoken word, film screenings, cultural exhibitions.
- **Family** — kids welcome, multi-generational, community fundraisers, school events.
- **Tonight** — anything happening in the next 6 hours. Time-urgent, highest engagement.

### Connect
Service providers, professionals, community resources. The "trusted referral" layer. Not "Islamic services" — professionals from the community who serve the community.

Sub-vibes within Connect:
- **Finance & legal** — halal mortgage, immigration law, financial planning
- **Home** — real estate, contractors, interior design
- **Health** — Muslim-friendly doctors, dentists, therapists
- **Creative** — photographers, designers, event planners (wedding season)
- **Learning** — tutors, Quran classes, professional development

### Share
Community posts, local tips, questions, recommendations. The user-generated layer. Feels like the community's group chat — but organized and discoverable.

Post types that fit this community:
- Food spots ("this place just opened and it's incredible")
- Event recaps ("last night's mixer was a vibe — follow them")
- Questions ("anyone know a good halal mortgage broker in Naperville?")
- Local news ("Mosque Foundation is doing free Iftar — pass it on")
- Recommendations ("my go-to spots in Schaumburg" thread)

## What the Halal Filter Means in Brand Terms

Halal certification is real and matters deeply to users. But how we surface it determines whether Muzgram feels like a religious compliance app or a trustworthy local guide.

**The principle: halal is quality assurance, not religious gatekeeping.**

A user who cares about halal doesn't want to be educated about it every time they open the app. They want to know: "Is this place the right kind of place?" in the same way any food app user wants to know "Is this place actually good?" — quickly and without explanation.

```
HOW HALAL STATUS APPEARS (correct approach)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Certified (IFANCA/ISNA):  Small emerald shield badge. That's it.
                           No copy. No explanation.
                           Tapping it opens a one-time education modal.
                           "This place is certified by IFANCA..."

Self-declared:             Small amber badge.
                           "Business says it's halal."
                           No shame, just information.

Unknown:                   No badge. Not visible unless user filters for it.
                           Never shown as "Not Halal" — we don't know.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The badge is like the Michelin star — you know what it means,
you don't need it explained every time.
```

## What "Social Ecosystem" Means in the Product

The previous framing positioned Muzgram primarily as a discovery utility. The correct framing is **discovery utility with social gravity** — the places, events, and moments that create a social ecosystem, not just a directory.

### Social Gravity Mechanics

**Trending Spots** — not algorithmic "we think you'll like this" — literally which spots have the most activity this week. "14 people from your area visited Noon O Kabab this week" is social proof that makes a restaurant feel alive, not just listed.

**Event Energy** — not just "47 people marked going" — the visual accumulation of interest. When an event has momentum (saves climbing, shares happening), the card should feel alive. A countdown that accelerates.

**The Scene Right Now** — the Live Now strip evolves from "events happening now" into "what's the actual energy in your neighborhood right now." A restaurant with a full house on a Friday night is different from one that's empty. The app should know the difference.

**Community Moments** — user-generated posts that capture a moment: "this dessert spot just opened and the line is already out the door." That post IS the social ecosystem. It makes the app feel like a living community bulletin board, not a database.

**Share to WhatsApp as the primary social action** — the Muslim community already has its social infrastructure. The WhatsApp group is the social network. Muzgram's job is to give those groups quality content to share. Every "Share" button is a word-of-mouth amplifier.

## Features That Change Tone With This Framing

### The Jummah Finder
**Previous framing:** "A dedicated Jummah screen — the mosque hub." Primary MMP feature.

**New framing:** Part of the Mosque Hub, which is one section of the community calendar. Jummah times are genuinely useful but they're utility, not personality. Present as: "Find a mosque near you" with practical info (times, language, distance). Not a centerpiece screen — a card in the Events section that surfaces on Fridays.

**The shift:** From a dedicated screen with religious weight → a useful filter in the event/community calendar that surfaces automatically on Fridays.

### Ramadan Mode
**Previous framing:** Religious event with Fajr timings, Taraweeh listings, Last 10 Nights section.

**New framing:** Ramadan is one of the most culturally vibrant times of year — the month everyone goes out for Iftar, the food scene peaks, events multiply, the community energy is electric. Ramadan mode amplifies the social and food discovery aspects first.

**What stays:** Iftar countdown in header (useful, not preachy), Ramadan specials category (food discovery), Suhoor spots (late-night discovery). 
**What changes:** Drop "Last 10 Nights" as a feature, drop "Taraweeh listings" as a primary focus — these are served by mosque-specific channels, not Muzgram.

### Prayer Time Awareness
**Previous framing:** "The app is prayer-aware — notifications suppressed during Maghrib."

**New framing:** This remains as silent infrastructure. The app respects prayer times by not blasting notifications at those moments. It is never mentioned to the user, never a feature, never marketed. It's just good taste.

### Community Groups
**Previous framing:** Mosque groups, neighborhood groups, interest groups.

**New framing:** Crew spaces. Groups are organized around social identity, not religious affiliation.
- "West Ridge Foodies" (interest: food)
- "Chicago Muslim Professionals" (interest: career)
- "Bridgeview Parents" (interest: family)
- "Devon Ave Regulars" (neighborhood + social)
- Mosque groups: still exist, run by mosque accounts, but presented as one type of group, not the primary type.

### People Match
**Previous framing:** "Community networking — not dating."

**New framing:** Find your crew. This is the 2026 version of "find people like you in your new city." Not romance, not formal networking — just the discovery that there are people with similar taste, in similar places, who you might vibe with. Culturally Muslim, same area, similar spots saved. The connection is left entirely to WhatsApp.

## The Visual Identity Implication

The design system (dark, warm, confident) is correct and doesn't need to change. But the visual content mix needs to reflect the social ecosystem identity:

```
VISUAL FEED SHOULD FEEL LIKE:
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
✓ A Friday night at a packed halal restaurant — warm light, energy
✓ A rooftop Eid party — people, lights, celebration
✓ A corner table at a chai spot — intimate, local, real
✓ The dessert that just arrived — close-up, craveable
✓ A community soccer game at dusk — active, outdoors, alive

NOT:
✗ Stock photo of a mosque silhouette at sunset
✗ Abstract Islamic geometric patterns as filler
✗ Formal event photography with banquet hall energy
✗ "Halal Certified" written in large text on photos
✗ Photos that could be from any generic religious community app
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

## The Anti-Pattern List

Every time one of these shows up in a feature, copy, or design decision, it needs to be called out:

```
RED FLAGS THAT SIGNAL "RELIGIOUS APP ENERGY"
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
✗ Any screen header that says "Islamic [anything]"
✗ Any push notification copy that includes "the Muslim community"
✗ Any onboarding that asks how religious the user is
✗ Any feature that's a "Muslim version of X" (Muslim LinkedIn, Muslim Yelp)
   — We are Muzgram. Not the Muslim version of something.
✗ Any content category called "Islamic Events"
✗ Any business category called "Islamic Services" or "Muslim-owned only"
✗ Any user-facing mention of "ummah" — that's for mosque communications
✗ Any "halal" in the primary navigation — it's a filter, not a tab
✗ Any religious reminder that users didn't explicitly ask for
✗ Any tone that implies users need guidance on how to be Muslim
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

## Summary

Muzgram is for a generation of Muslims who are fully, proudly themselves — and who have never had an app that was built for them without either ignoring their identity or making it the only thing about them.

The design is dark and cool. The feed is alive with things happening near them. The community is their actual crew — not a sanitized idea of what a "Muslim community" should look like. The events are real. The restaurants are places they'd actually go. The language sounds like the friend who always knows where to go.

**That is the product. Every decision flows from here.**
